Programs

Youth Program (Each One Reach One)

Engaging youth from diverse communities in Mentorship, Skill Building Programs that will promote Youth Development, Empowerment and Philanthropy.

Our overall mission is to engage youth to build a volunteer base in the city of Vaughan. WEConnect plans to engage youth and seniors from Vaughan area and its neighbourhood and give them a platform to socially engage in activities that will promote their development, empowerment and Philanthropy in these young residents while engaging seniors in active life, sharing their experiences and cultural heritage with youth. Program will link youth with seniors who are either living with them or participants of our programs or senior residents in their neighbourhoods or senior care facilities.

Our goal is to educate and make youth aware of needs of elderly people, to get engaged in the activities of daily life with them, learn new skills. To know what is elder abuse and neglect, and how youth can play a positive role in the lives of seniors with whom they interact.

Our first step will be to approach school boards/ settlement counsellors and foster partnership with them. We will organise information sessions on related topics for students and encourage or invite them to participate in our "EACH ONE REACH ONE"‐ program, invest time in their communities, work to help seniors/ diverse communities and gain volunteer experience. They will be able to complete their mandatory 40 hours of volunteer time as well they will learn new skills. Arranging workshops on budgeting, time management, seniors care, senior’s health and safety, resources in the community etc. Fun and skill building programs could also be made available to students. These workshops will be supported by partners with expertise in financial management, seniors health and care and life skills.

By building a youth volunteer base and providing them with opportunities will help high school students complete their mandatory volunteer hours needed for graduation. The outcome of the engagement of youth with seniors is providing an intergenerational exchange. This exchange will lighten spirits of the seniors, as well as help build new skills and intrinsic values in the youth who engage by showing them a broader perspective of life. This also helps build responsibility, increase their self‐esteem and give students a stronger sense of belonging when they engage in their communities.

WEConnect will foster strong partnerships with school boards and school settlement and social workers in order to reach out to the students and their parents.

Seniors Program

Program 1: Recreational and Spiritual Activities

Our approach to wellbeing is by having activities and programs that focus on recreation, spiritual and physical wellbeing of seniors and women. By engaging their mind, bodies in an environment where they feel good about who they are and what they can / have achieved in this beautiful country.

Activities like Yoga, meditation, art classes and singing competition, playing board games and cards, sharing their experiences and beautiful memories within the group or with youth, Sharing old recipes and other expertise with younger generation, awareness programs about health and safety, senior’s abuse are some of the programs that will embrace, engage , empower and integrate our seniors in our communities.

Program 2: Special Needs

To encourage seniors who are dealing with memory issues like dementia, Alzheimer’s and are unable to go for specific day programs to join our group where they will be provided with activation and recreation programs by trained professionals. It could be a two hour program for seniors and their care givers. Required information sessions will also be arranged for seniors and their family members.

Program 3: Training Programs

Encouraging, enabling and training seniors to volunteer their expertise and time for the community hence remain physically and socially active.

As we are dealing with diverse communities in terms of culture, social class, gender, people with disabilities, immigration status, visible minority, the majorities, the natives, our main focus shall remain to build inclusive communities, our programs be open to all. The importance of recognizing and acknowledging different individual needs we shall ensure (to the best of our capacity) equity in terms of access, participation, and program benefits for one and all individuals and families. Membership is open to all irrespective of their gender, marital status, sexual orientation, religious beliefs, age, disability, race etc. Our programs and services are diversity focussed and we adhere to the values of anti‐oppression and anti‐racism

Economic Development and Integration

To provide an opportunity to women from diverse communities utilize/share their skills and become self‐reliant.

Identify women who have mastery over or knowledge of Art Work such as Bead Work, Embroidery & Mirror Work and other ethnic artistry and are willing to develop / use this skill / hobby into a source of income. (Product developed out of this joint activity will be displayed and put on sale, the profit could be distributed amongst the participants.

Arrange information sessions on small scale business, how to register, budgeting, marketing skills etc. This will help women who are not ready for Canadian job market to utilize their time and skills and turn it into a source of income

The client target group includes Canadian Citizens, Permanent Residents, Refugees and Refugee Claimants, particularly children, youth, women, seniors living in City of Vaughan and its neighbourhood.

Women's Networking Group

Our programs shall promote gender equality and women empowerment. This program aims to help women become full participants in Canadian life capable of achieving their aspirations and potential thereby facilitating their full integration into Canadian system. Such programs will increase awareness levels and build self‐reliance skills, will enable women have increased knowledge of, and know how to access community services helping them to move towards financial independence. Group activities/ information sessions will be organized to expand newcomer WOMEN'S knowledge of her new community as well as her new country, available resources, her rights and responsibilities including her civic responsibilities as Vaughan resident. Celebrating with them their ethnic festivals as well as Canadian cultural days will help them understand their new country better, adapting to Canadian culture while keeping their own cultural heritage intact will create awareness and image building in new home of Canada/Vaughan.

Sessions making women aware of their legal rights and addressing issues of common concern such as abuse, sponsorship breakdown, inter‐generational conflict and other family related issues will be some of the programs to empower women. Free legal clinics will be organised for women who are dealing with separation, divorce and child custody.
